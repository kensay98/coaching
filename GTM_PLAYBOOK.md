- Active on Twitter/X and Telegram (not LinkedIn)
- Bilingual practice: English + Russian
- Beautiful website, but no acquisition engine
- Dual-track positioning: founders (5-100) + ambitious leaders
- Resend chosen for future email infrastructure

## PHASE 1: FIRST 5 CLIENTS (Weeks 1-8)

### 1.1 Warm Outreach (Weeks 1-2) — Get your first 2-3 clients

Your fastest path. People who already know and trust you.

**Action list:**
1. **Make a list of 30-50 people** from your network:
   - Founders (any stage)
   - Leaders stepping into bigger roles
   - People who've mentioned leadership struggles
   - Former colleagues now managing teams
   - Split: ~half EN-speaking, ~half RU-speaking

2. **Send personal messages** (Telegram DMs, Twitter DMs, WhatsApp — wherever you naturally talk to them):

   **Founder version:**\n
   > "Hey [Name], I've been thinking about you. I coach founders through the hard decisions and the people stuff you can't talk about with your team or investors. Given what you're dealing with at [company], thought it might be useful. I offer a free first session — not a sales call, actual coaching. Want to try it?"

   **Leader version:**\n
   > "Hey [Name], I've been thinking about you. I coach ambitious leaders who are carrying hard decisions and navigating people dynamics. Given what you're dealing with at [company], thought it might be useful. I offer a free first session — not a sales call, actual coaching. Want to try it?"

   For RU contacts:
   **Фаундер:**\n
   > "Привет [Name], давно думал тебе написать. Я коучу фаундеров — по сути, быть thinking partner'ом для того, о чём нельзя поговорить с командой или инвесторами. Учитывая что у тебя происходит в [company], подумал что может быть полезно. Первая сессия бесплатная — не продажа, реальный коучинг. Хочешь попробовать?"

   **Лидер:**\n
   > "Привет [Name], давно думал тебе написать. Я коучу лидеров, которые несут сложные решения и постоянно в конфликте интересов. Учитывая что у тебя происходит в [company], подумал что может быть полезно. Первая сессия бесплатная — не продажа, реальный коучинг. Хочешь попробовать?"

3. **Goal:** 15-20 conversations → 8-10 free sessions → 3-4 paying clients

**Key principles:**
- Message the person, not the role. Reference something real.
- Don't apologize for reaching out. You're offering something valuable.
- Don't pitch. Invite.
- If someone says "not now" — "Is there someone you know who might benefit?"

### 1.2 Twitter/X Cold Start (Weeks 1-4) — English engine

Twitter rewards sharpness, honesty, and a distinct voice. Here's how to build from zero:

**Week 1: Setup**
- Bio: Keep it tight. Something like: "Coach founders + ambitious leaders through the internal stuff. Building @ttune_ai. Former [credibility signal]."
- Pinned tweet: Your "why" story — why you coach, who you serve, link to free session
- Follow 100-200 accounts: founders, VCs, indie hackers, people in the build-in-public space

**Week 1-2: Start posting (1-2x/day)**

Twitter is different from LinkedIn. Posts should be:
- **Short and sharp** (1-5 sentences for single tweets)
- **Threads for depth** (4-8 tweets when you have something real to unpack)
- **No corporate tone.** Write like you'd text a smart friend.
- **Contrarian > consensus.** Challenge what founders think they know.

Example tweets:

> "The hardest conversation in your startup isn't with your board or your team. It's the one you're avoiding with yourself."

> "Founders don't need more advice. They need someone who'll sit with them in the discomfort of not knowing."

> "Leadership isn't about confidence. It's about being able to make a call while your body is screaming."

> "'I just need to work harder' is the most expensive lie in startups."

> "Your body knows you're about to lose your best engineer before your mind does. Most founders have forgotten how to listen to it."

> Thread: "I coach founders 1:1. Here are the 5 patterns I see over and over — and none of them are about strategy:"
> 1/ Saying yes when they mean no...
> 2/ Carrying everything alone because 'that's what founders do'...
> (etc.)

**Week 3-4: Build the flywheel**
- Reply to founder tweets with genuine, thoughtful takes (not "this!" or "great point")
- Quote-tweet interesting founder struggles with your perspective
- Engage with 10-15 tweets/day from people in your ICP
- Post 1-2x daily, 1 thread per week

**Content categories (rotate):**
1. **Sharp observations** — one-liner truths about founder life
2. **Threads** — deeper dives into patterns, methodology, stories
3. **Personal** — your own founder journey, what you're learning
4. **Methodology demystified** — what somatic work / parts work actually looks like (make it concrete, not woo)
5. **Reframes** — flip conventional startup wisdom

**What NOT to tweet:**
- Motivational platitudes
- "10 tips" threads
- Anything that sounds like a life coach Instagram post
- Direct "hire me" pitches
- Engagement bait ("agree?", "retweet if...")

### 1.3 Telegram Strategy (Weeks 1-4) — Russian engine

Two parallel moves:

**A) Join existing founder communities**
- Find 3-5 active Telegram groups where RU-speaking founders hang out
- Startup communities, tech leadership groups, CIS founder networks
- Lurk for 1 week, then start contributing — answer questions about leadership, team dynamics, founder struggles
- Share perspectives, not pitches
- After 2-3 weeks of genuine contribution, people check your profile → discover coaching

**B) Start your own Telegram channel**
- Name it something that captures your angle (not "Coaching by [Name]" — something that speaks to the reader's problem)
- Post 2-3x per week: longer-form reflections, observations, frameworks
- This is your Russian-language "blog" — deeper than tweets, more personal
- Cross-promote: mention it in relevant group conversations when your content is genuinely relevant
- Goal: 100-300 subscribers in first 2 months (quality over quantity — these are potential clients)

**Telegram content examples:**

> "Вчера на сессии фаундер сказал: 'Я знаю что мне нужно уволить СТО, но я не могу — он мой друг.' Это не про дружбу. Это про страх конфликта, который маскируется под лояльность. И это один из самых дорогих паттернов в стартапах."

> "Медитация — это не про расслабление. Для фаундера это тренировка: сидеть в неопределённости и не дёргаться. Самый полезный навык, которому никто не учит."

> "Разница между ментором и коучем: ментор даёт свой ответ. Коуч помогает тебе услышать свой. Большинству фаундеров не нужно больше чужих мнений — им нужно пространство, чтобы услышать собственное."

### 1.4 Free Sessions as Product (Ongoing)

Your free session IS your sales process.

**Before the session:**
- Cal.com pre-booking form with:
  - "What's the one thing keeping you up at night about your role?"
  - "What would make this session valuable for you?"

**During the session (60 min):**
- First 5 min: "This is real coaching, not a pitch. Let's work on something real."
- Next 45 min: Actually coach. Go deep. Full toolkit.
- Last 10 min: "What was this like? What surprised you?"
- Do NOT pitch. Instead: "If this felt useful and you want to keep going, I can share how I work with clients. No pressure — take a few days."

**After the session (within 24h):**
> "Thanks for the session today. I noticed [specific observation]. That's the kind of thing that shifts when you have a consistent space to work on it.
>
> If you'd like to keep going, here's how it works: we meet weekly (60min) with Telegram support between sessions. I use a sliding scale — you pick what fits, no questions asked:
>
> **$150/mo** — things are tight right now. Pre-revenue or making very little.
> **$250/mo** — bootstrapping or early-stage. Watching every dollar.
> **$450/mo** — have funding, revenue, or a salary. Where most clients land.
> **$700/mo** — strong position. Helps keep the lower tiers available.
>
> Same coaching at every price. Minimum commitment is 3 months, then month-to-month.
>
> Choose what fits. No rush — take a few days to sit with it."

Let them sit with it for 2-3 days before following up.

**Conversion target:** 40-50% of free sessions → paying clients

## PHASE 2: SCALE TO 10-12 (Weeks 8-16)

### 2.1 Referral System (Starting Week 6)

**When to ask:** Month 2-3 of a client's engagement.

**How to ask:**
> "Is there someone in your world — another founder, tech lead — dealing with similar stuff? Not a formal referral, just... if someone comes to mind who could use this kind of space, I'd be happy to offer them a free session."

**After engagement ends:**
> "Whenever you run into a founder stuck on the people/leadership stuff, feel free to send them my way."

**Key:** Never offer discounts. It cheapens the premium positioning. The referral itself is the value.

**Expected yield:** Each happy client refers 1-2 people over 6 months.

### 2.2 Twitter Flywheel (Ongoing)

By week 8 you should have:
- 50-80 tweets posted
- Some tweets that hit (50+ likes, meaningful replies)
- A growing follower base of founders

**Level up:**
1. **Double down on what resonated.** Check your analytics. Write more of what works.
2. **Start threads with client stories** (anonymized): "A founder I work with realized that every time he said 'I'm fine' in a meeting, his team heard 'don't bring me problems.'"
3. **Engage with bigger accounts.** Reply thoughtfully to founders with 10k-100k followers. Their audience sees your replies.
4. **Soft CTAs in threads:** End threads with "I have 2 spots opening next month. If you're a founder dealing with [specific thing], DM me."
5. **Twitter Spaces:** Join or host a Space on founder mental health / leadership. Great for visibility and real-time connection.

### 2.3 Telegram Flywheel (Ongoing)

**Your channel should now have 100-300 subscribers.**

**Level up:**
1. Cross-post your best content from Twitter (translated/adapted, not copy-pasted)
2. Do a monthly live Q&A in your channel or in a founder group
3. Collaborate with other RU-speaking Telegram channels — cross-promote with founders, VCs, tech leaders who have their own channels
4. Start collecting testimonials in Russian and post them (with permission)

### 2.4 YouTube (Starting Week 10)

**Why now:** You have 2 months of content and ideas. You know what resonates. Time to go deeper.

**Format options (pick one to start):**
- **Solo talks** (5-15 min): Expand your best Twitter threads into video. Camera on, no fancy editing. Raw and real.
- **Conversations**: Record conversations with founders (not coaching sessions — topical discussions about founder life)

**Cadence:** 2x/month. Quality over quantity. YouTube is a long game.

**Why YouTube works for coaching:**
- Video builds trust faster than text. People feel like they know you.
- A founder who watches 3 of your videos before booking a free session is basically pre-sold.
- YouTube content is evergreen — a video from month 3 still brings leads in month 12.
- Bilingual option: do some in English, some in Russian. Or subtitle.

### 2.5 Podcast Guesting (Starting Week 10)

**How to get booked:**
1. List 20 podcasts with <5k downloads (easier to get on, engaged audiences)
   - Founder journey pods, leadership pods, startup pods
   - Both EN and RU language podcasts
2. Pitch: "I coach startup founders and I'm also building ttune.ai. I have a take on why founders struggle with 'the people stuff' and what actually helps — it's not what most people think."
3. Prep your "signature story" — one compelling narrative that demonstrates your approach
4. Goal: 1-2 appearances/month

## PHASE 3: CAPACITY + WAITLIST (Weeks 16-24)

### 3.1 Capacity Management

**When you hit 10 clients:**
- Update Twitter bio: "Executive coach for founders | Currently full, DM for waitlist"
- Update Telegram channel pinned post: waitlist info
- Website: swap CTA from "Book Free Session" to "Join Waitlist"
- Keep posting — content doesn't stop when you're full. It builds the waitlist.

**When a spot opens:**
- Email/message next person on waitlist
- Scarcity is real, not manufactured — you can only serve 12 people well at weekly cadence

### 3.2 Pricing: Self-Selecting Sliding Scale

A sliding scale with four fixed price points. No tier names. Same coaching for everyone — clients pick what fits.

| | $150/mo | $250/mo | $450/mo | $700/mo |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Who** | Making under $3K/mo, real financial hardship, very early CIS/RU founders | Bootstrapped, pre-revenue, reinvesting everything | Funded, have revenue or salary | Strong financial position, want to support accessibility |
| **Sessions** | 4/month (weekly, 60min) | 4/month (weekly, 60min) | 4/month (weekly, 60min) | 4/month (weekly, 60min) |
| **Async support** | Telegram between sessions | Telegram between sessions | Telegram between sessions | Telegram between sessions |
| **Coaching quality** | Full | Full | Full | Full |

**$150 tier cap:** 1-2 clients max at this price. Keeps the model sustainable while being truly accessible.

#### The Math

| Scenario | $150 | $250 | $450 | $700 | Total/month |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Early phase** (6 clients) | 1 × $150 | 2 × $250 | 2 × $450 | 1 × $700 | **$2,250** |
| **Conservative** (10 clients) | 2 × $150 | 3 × $250 | 3 × $450 | 2 × $700 | **$3,800** |
| **Balanced** (12 clients) | 1 × $150 | 3 × $250 | 5 × $450 | 3 × $700 | **$5,250** |
| **Strong** (12 clients) | 1 × $150 | 2 × $250 | 5 × $450 | 4 × $700 | **$5,600** |
| **Mostly mid-tier** (12 clients) | 2 × $150 | 3 × $250 | 5 × $450 | 2 × $700 | **$4,700** |

#### Client-Facing Message (send after free session)

> **How pricing works:**
>
> I use a sliding scale. You pick what fits — no questions asked.
>
> **$150/mo** — things are tight right now. You're pre-revenue or making very little. This exists so money isn't the reason you don't get support.
>
> **$250/mo** — you're bootstrapping or early-stage. Investing in yourself but watching every dollar.
>
> **$450/mo** — you have funding, revenue, or a salary. This is where most clients land.
>
> **$700/mo** — you're in a strong position. Choosing this helps keep the lower tiers available.
>
> Same coaching at every price. Same sessions, same depth, same me.

#### Pricing Escalation Over Time

Only applies to **new clients**. Never raise prices on existing clients mid-engagement.

| Phase | When | $150 | $250 | $450 | $700 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1** (clients 1-5) | Weeks 1-8 | $150 | $250 | $450 | $700 |
| **Phase 2** (clients 6-10) | Weeks 8-16 | $150 (keep) | $300 | $500 | $800 |
| **Phase 3** (10-12 + waitlist) | Weeks 16-24 | $200 | $350 | $600 | $900 |

The $150 tier stays fixed longest — it's the accessibility anchor. At Phase 3 rates with 12 clients (1/3/5/3 split): $200 + $1,050 + $3,000 + $2,700 = **$6,950/month**.

#### Engagement Structure

- **Minimum commitment:** 3 months (12 sessions)
- **Payment:** Monthly (not per-session — creates consistency)
- **Renewal:** Month-to-month after initial 3 months
- **Cancellation:** 2 weeks notice

Why 3-month minimum: real results take 8-12 sessions, it protects income stability, and signals serious work. Clients who commit for 3 months are far more likely to stay 6-12.

#### Scenario Playbook

**CIS/RU founder, very early, clearly tight on money:**
Guide them to $150 — "I have a couple spots at $150/month for founders in exactly your situation." Frame it as normal, not charity.

**Bootstrapped founder, has some revenue but watching every dollar:**
$250 is their natural fit — "Most bootstrapped founders go with $250."

**US/EU founder, Series A, clearly funded:**
They'll likely self-select $450 or $700. If they pick $250, that's their choice — honor system means no policing.

**Someone asks "what do most people pay?":**
"Most clients pay $450/month. Pick whatever fits your situation."

**Someone wants to negotiate below $150:**
"I hear you. $150 is the lowest I can offer — it's subsidized by clients paying more. If that doesn't work right now, I'm happy to revisit when things change."

**Existing client's company gets funded / grows:**
Don't bring it up proactively. If they offer to move up, accept gracefully. The honor system only works if you actually honor it.

**At 10+ clients and someone wants $150:**
Be selective — "I currently have limited spots. I can offer $250 or $450. If that's a stretch, I'll add you to the waitlist for when a $150 spot opens."

#### What NOT to Do

- **Don't publish prices on your website.** Share pricing after the free session, in a personal message. Prevents price-shopping without context.
- **Don't offer discounts.** The sliding scale IS the discount. No additional negotiation.
- **Don't offer packages with bulk session discounts.** Monthly is clean. Keep it simple.
- **Don't charge per session.** Monthly subscription creates commitment and predictability.
- **Don't explain or justify the model too much.** State it simply, let the framing do the work.

### 3.3 Waitlist System (Simple)

Manual version (no website changes needed):
1. **Google Form**: Name, Email, Role, "What are you dealing with?", How they found you
2. **Google Sheet**: Track the queue
3. When a spot opens, message the next person
4. No response in 48h → move to next

Later: integrate into website with Resend for automated emails.
