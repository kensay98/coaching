The process

How it works

1

For Important Decisions

  • Articulate the problem out loud — chaos turns into clarity

2

For Working with Patterns

  • Notice the pattern — separate "this is me" from "this is coincidence"

  • Practice — the new becomes normal

Objections

Common concerns

"I should handle this myself"

Admitting you need a thinking partner isn't weakness. It's what the best leaders in the world do. The higher you go, the fewer people you can be honest with.

"How is this different from a therapist or mentor?"

A mentor gives advice from their experience — you get their answer, not yours. A therapist works with diagnoses and the past. A coach is a thinking partner: helps find your answers without having their own stake.

"I'm worried information will leak"

Complete confidentiality. No connections to your investors, board, or competitors. Ever. This is foundational to the work.

"I don't have time"

One session per week, flexible schedule. The clarity from one good decision saves more time than you'll invest. The cost of a bad decision is far higher.

Alternatives

Why not...

"Talk to a mentor"

A mentor has their own opinion and often their own interest. You'll get their answer, not yours. And you can't be completely honest about your fears and doubts.

"Go to a therapist"

Therapy is about "not okay → okay". Working with the past, diagnoses, childhood. You don't need treatment — you need a partner for the next level.

"Discuss with co-founder, partner, or friend"

They're too close. You can't tell them everything. And they'll either get scared or start giving advice based on incomplete information.

"Handle it myself, as always"

You've been procrastinating that decision for days. The pattern that cost you your third key employee won't change on its own. What got you here won't get you there.
