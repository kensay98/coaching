Where you might be now

From the outside, things look fine. You've built something. You're capable. You're successful by most measures.

Inside, it's messier:

  • Decisions pile up because you can't think through them clearly, or you're scared of being wrong
  • The same patterns keep showing up in how you lead and how you react under stress — visible but unstoppable
  • You're performing "strong" when inside you feel like a fraud
  • You feel alone with the hard stuff — the fears, the doubts, the questions you can't ask anyone
  • You sense there's more, but you don't know how to get there

What can change

  • Clarity. That decision you've been avoiding? You'll see it clearly, including your blind spots, and make a call you can stand behind.
  • Different patterns. The behaviors that keep costing you — relationships, opportunities, sleep. You'll understand why they're there and find another way.
  • Steadiness. The ability to stay calm when things are hard. Leading from who you are, not who you're performing to be.
  • Not being alone. Someone who knows who you actually are, beyond the role, and holds that without judgment.
  • Growth. Becoming the leader you need to be for what's next, without losing yourself.
Why This

Why coaching, not something else

vs. Mentor or Advisor

They give you their answer, from their experience, with their biases. You can't be fully honest about fears or doubts — they're evaluating whether you're "leadership material."

Coaching gives you your answer — uncovered through questions and challenge. An answer you own because it came from you.

vs. Therapist

Therapy takes you from "not okay" to "okay." Important work, and if that's what you need, do that.

This is "okay" to "next level." You're already functioning. We're working on evolution, not repair.

vs. Friends, Partner, Co-founder

They're too close. They have stakes in your decisions. You can't say "I'm afraid I can't handle this" without consequences.

I have zero stake in your decisions. Complete confidentiality. Nothing you say will be shared with anyone or used against you.

vs. Other Coaches

Many business coaches stay surface-level — tactics and frameworks. Many "transformational" coaches are abstract and slow.

I integrate both. Depth modalities with the pragmatism of someone who's actually built things in tech.

How It Works

Format & approach

Format

  • 1:1 sessions, 60 minutes each
  • Every two weeks, adjusted to what's happening in your life
  • 3-6 months is typical
  • No curriculum. You bring what's on your mind. The stuff that matters to you right now is where the work happens.

How I Work

We work with whatever you bring. Sometimes that's a concrete decision you need to make this week. Sometimes it goes deeper — into the patterns and beliefs underneath. I draw from several approaches to go wherever the session needs to go:

  • ICF coaching fundamentals — listening, asking questions, creating space for you to think clearly and find your own answers
  • Body awareness — patterns show up in your body before your mind catches up. Sometimes we need to go there.
  • Parts work (IFS) — understanding the competing voices inside you instead of fighting them
  • Vipassana — shapes how I show up: the ability to sit with discomfort without rushing to fix it
